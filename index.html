Step into the world of Virtual Reality

Let's explore some of the ways virtual reality enhances the museum experience

What is VR Technology?

Virtual reality is a term that is used for describing the 3D, computer generated environments that can be explored and interacted with by a person.

How do we use it?

A range of equipment is used such as headsets, special gloves and omni-directional treadmills that will be used to stimulate the senses in order to create an illusion of reality.


Samsung Gear Virtual Reality Headset

Virtual reality technology is stepping its game up in the world of entertainment and discovery.

Let's explore some of the ways virtual reality has changed how we experience and learn about the world around us.

Coca Cola’s Santa’s Virtual Reality Sleigh Ride

In 2015, Cocal Cola had launched a virtual reality experience of a sleigh ride using the Oculus Rift technology. Thousands of people in Poland had the opportunity to immerse themselves into a virtual world and become Santa Claus. People were able to imagine what it would be like to travel around the world in a sleigh over counrties and villages.

Watch the video

Coca-Cola have been experimenting with Virtual Reality for a while now and are striving to be new and fresh at all times. This sleigh ride was able to show consumers that the brand stays modern with the use of the VR experience. It was a great way to get people to experience and enjoy the feel of Christmas.

Virtual Reality Heritage Sites

Virtual reality has been used to construct virtual walkthroughs of historical heritage sites. With the use of interactive technology such as virtual reality, museums are able to open up space for a new audience and educate visitors with a more personal experience. Children are a big part of success in interactive displays as they find it often boring to simply browse museum exhibits. However, the use of virtual reality allows museums to attract them to experience history in a much more fun way.

Other uses of Virtual Reality

Looking at how virtual reality can change the way we use museums, let's not forget that it is also very useful for other things in our society too.

Healthcare

Virtual reality use in healthcare has been a big part since the ability to create surgery simulations, phobia treatment, skill training and even robotic surgery has become a reality.

One of the many advantages of using virtual reality technology for medicine is that doctors, nurses and others can learn new skills and even revisit and refresh old skills in an environment that is safe and does not bring harm to anyone.

Business

From testing products before production to training new employees, virtual reality in the business world has a great deal that it can offer it.

It is extremely useful for business trip meetings, conference calls and overall communication with various location employees. This allows an international company for example to always keep in touch with their branches around the world.

Education

For children, learning is a key part of their life, and virtual reality can help them learn with more visual and more memorable information rather than just textbook reading.

Having experiences for historical events, space exploration or even 3D object modelling can help those who might be better at visual learning using colour, sounds and textures.
